Overview

In light of the recent COVID-19 landscape, business and technology have been forced to adapt rapidly to remote means in order to remain functional. The general K-12 education system has faced immense pressure and challenge to quickly adapt the centuries old in-person model to virtual or distance models of schooling. Our team worked with Avanade to improve upon a sector within the education system that relies on e-learning, specifically first grade.

In coming up with a solution, I was to consider and understand the current methods, tools, pain points, and gaps from students, teachers, staff, parents, and anyone else who plays an important role in the transition to e-learning.

Our project goals were the following:

Understand the transition from in person learning to e-learning

Identify gaps in current tools and resources being used for communication between stakeholders

Build upon opportunities and pain points in the e-learning space

Final Concept

Towards the end of our project, our group created a set of mockups highlighting our solution as a Zoom plugin. This plugin was designed by our team with features that would support first grade teachers' specific needs.
We broke it down into the following four categories:

Highlighting important features For our concept of making important features more visible, we added the raise hand, yes, and no buttons to the main toolbar for students.

New breakout room format For our concept of creating a new breakout room format, we made it so the teacher can see all the breakout rooms on one screen along with a video feed.

Receive feedback from students For helping teachers receive feedback from students, we created a simple way for 1st graders to say how they are feeling about class content.

Keep students engaged Students being distracted is something that teachers have difficulty dealing with in e-learning. To alleviate this, we designed a way for teachers to keep students engaged over Zoom by sending them an attention grabber.

Our Process

Scoping

As the entire K-12 learning sphere would be too large and unfocused of a scope, our team had to ultimately decide upon a problem scope that would allow us to target specific problems and pain points. Through some literary research and preliminary interviews, we decided to give our full attention to the first grade learning space, specifically designing for first grade teachers. These methods gave us the required insight to narrow down.

  • Due to familial and personal discrepancies, designing for teachers would be more more valuble than attempting to cater to each student

  • Individual attention is more valuble at this age
  • First graders have less variation in overall development at their age



  • Younger students generally have a tougher time adapting to the e-learning environment

Interviews

During this project, I made a drastic change in my mental approach to ethnographic practices. I personally used to heavily stray away from these methods, such as observations or interviews because I would get nervous, feel unprepared, and would lack skills to create an open dialouge in a comfortable manner. These were personal predicaments that needed to change to further myself in improving my UX research skills. I assumed the role of facilitating, managing, and conducting stakeholder interviews to tackle this.

After 11 interviews with teachers, parents, and administrators, my biggest personal takeaway was in fact my increasing fondness and appreciation of this process. An understanding that people love to talk about what they love motivated me to push through each interview, slowly focusing on the conversation and the in-the-moment learning as opposed to constantly being in stress about the information that we need and the goals we have to meet.

The interview rounds proved to be extremely vital as our project was built around the first hand information and insights stakeholders could provide us.

Interview Goals

  • Understanding the workflow and barriers for teachers as they transition to e-learning
  • Understand how student-teacher interactions occur before and after the switch
  • Compile a list of postive and negative contrasts in conducting teaching before and after the switch

Interview Insights

  • Situational, technical, and learning constraints put up challenges for parents, students, and teachers
  • A loss in the amount of social interaction causes a lack of engagement
  • The structure of learning was negatively affected with the lack of learning activities that promoted development /li>

Ideation

Our ideation was broken into themes according to interview insights. For much of the preliminary ideation rounds, we stuck to simple paper and whiteboarding sketches to design simple systems that could be integrated in and improve a first-grade teachers workflow. The sketch themes included tools that helped teachers assess their students, improving upon existing online resources, and methods to facilitate social interactions.

    Some Insights:

  • Physical tools were ruled out because of the socio-economic discrepancy between many elementary schools
  • Improving upon and filling gaps in an existing service held the most merit as it poses the least resistance to be embedded in a teachers workflow

Concept Building + Evaluation

We chose zoom as a platform to design a plugin upon since it was used by the majority of teachers we talked to, as well as supported the use of plugins. After synthesizing the interviews and ideation rounds, we came up with a set of categories that we could build our final concept off of, and met with additional first grade teachers to evaluate our ideas. Each of the categories designs upon the four most prominent pain points in a first grade teachers experience, and were finalized after the feedback from the teachers.

Highlighting important features to tailor to first grade students

New breakout room format

New method of receiving feedback from students

New method of keeping students engaged
